Menstruation Festival - P4 Positive P4 Period: Kolkata, India

Andrea was invited to share a personal Jewish story in Kolkata’s second annual menstruation festival to break persisting taboos and stigmas around menstruation in India and around the world. The all-day virtual event moved west from Singapore to Hawaii, gathering Indian and foreign tellers to exchange stories and cultures. Andrea invited Indian tellers from the festival to American venues, and their relationships continue.

“Thanks for the courage you displayed to speak on such a “taboo” topic”

-Proyanka Chatterjee, Arts India Council

Adventures in Lifelong Learning at Framingham State University

Course 102: Making the Old New: Personal Storytelling with Traditional Material

Andrea taught a four-course session to older adults combining academic history with interactive workshopping, culminating with preparing a story for performance at a real venue. This class was prepared to be in-person and went virtual as one of the university’s first virtual offerings for isolated seniors during the pandemic.

“C.’s performance last night provided him an opportunity to develop and apply his creativity, develop new communication practices, and socially engage. I am grateful to you for offering him and the others in your course the opportunity to take risks in a safe place.”

Anne M. Robarti, Director of English Language Programs and Community Education
